About Me

Currently, I am a Master’s student at Southeast University, advised by Prof. Mingxing Hu. I received my B.S. degree from Central South University, and I hope to pursue a Ph.D. in the future. My research interests lie in human mobility, daily activity space vitality, and health-related urban issues. Methodologically, I work with machine learning, spatial analysis, econometrics, and GIS.

Outside research, I would describe myself as energetic and open-minded. I value both focus and balance: when working toward a goal, I can stay highly concentrated for a period of time, and once a major task is completed, I enjoy taking time to recharge and explore life beyond work. If MBTI says anything about me, I am probably an ENFP.

I also enjoy photography. If you share similar interests, I would be very happy to connect and become friends.

Open to academic exchange and collaboration. If you are interested in my work, please feel free to contact me via email: 220230067@seu.edu.cn.

Research Directions

Human Mobility and Spatial Interaction

I view the city as a network and people as dynamic actors rather than static individuals fixed in one place. From this perspective, I am interested in how different population groups, especially vulnerable groups such as older adults, interact with urban space through movement, activity patterns, and everyday spatial practices.

Environmental Health and GeoML

I am also interested in environmental health, especially in understanding how the built environment affects air pollution and related health risks. In this line of work, I use spatial machine learning approaches to capture spatial heterogeneity and nonlinearity, with the goal of supporting healthier and more evidence-based urban planning.
